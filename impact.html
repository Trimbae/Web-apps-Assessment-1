IMPACT

The technological advancement that Sergey Brin and Larry Page made when they created the search engine Google would revolutionise the way we consume content on the internet for years to come. Google made pages on the internet accessible to the layman, it "indexed the internet extraordinarily well... and in such a way that the user did not have to know how to use the index or Boolean search methods"[1].

From this base Google, with Brin spearheading many projects, went on to make many significant advancements in the field of data and computing - contuining to make information on the internet more and more accessible to the everyday user.

Advancements [2]
Year Event
1996 Brin and Page collaberate on search engine called BackRub, the backbone for Google.
1998 Google is founded
2000 Google Toolbar allows users to search without going to the Google homepage
2001 Google Images launches, giving users access to hundreds of millions of images from around the internet
2005 Google Maps is launched, allowing users to access maps from around the world all in one place, soon after this satellite images are added
2006 Google Translate launches enabling translation between over 70 different languages in an instant
2007 Google Streetview is launched, allowing users to see street-level 360 degree views of cities around the world
2007 Google announces Android, the first open platform operating system for mobile devices, paving the way for many advancements in smartphone technology
2008 Google Suggest (later renamed Autocomplete) is launched helping users formulate queries and also reducing time and keystrokes when searching using Google
2008 Google Chrome is released, a fast and lightweight internet browser that now boasts over 750 million users
2010 Google release their phone the Nexus One to showcase the capability of their Android OS
2010 Google Instant shows you reults for the user's predicted query instantly as they type
2010 Google announces they are developing technology that will allow cars to drive themselves in an effort to reduce accidents and emissions of normal cars
2012 Google's 'Project Glass' is unveiled, a pair of 'smart' glasses that allow users to consume media, take pictures and perform various other tasks
2012 Installation of Google Fiber begins, a revolutionary super-high speed fibre optic broadband 100x faster than average broadband
2010 Google make their 'Compute Engine' generally available, allowing developers to build and host applications on Google's infrastructure
